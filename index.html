Wake-Up Flow · 20-25 min · daily

Move with your breath. Slow beats deep. This undoes the desk.

01
Cat-Cow x 10
Breathe: inhale as you arch, exhale as you round. Wrong if your low back pinches; shrink the range.
02
Thread the Needle x 6/side
Reach far, let the upper back twist. Wrong if the stretch lands in your shoulder instead of between the shoulder blades.
03
World's Greatest Stretch x 5/side
Long lunge, hand inside the front foot, rotate and reach up. Breathe: exhale into the rotation.
04
Downward Dog to Cobra x 8
Breathe: exhale into Dog, inhale through to Cobra. Heels reach down; hips stay relaxed in Cobra.
05
Neck circles + chin tucks x 10
Make a double chin, feel the back of the neck lengthen. Wrong if you feel strain at the front of the throat.
06
Hip flexor stretch · 45 sec/side
Squeeze the glute of the back leg to deepen it. Sitting shortens these; this is the antidote.
07
Dead hang or chest stretch · 30-45 sec
Hang relaxed, slow nose breathing. Decompresses the spine after the flow.

Sprints · Tue / Thu / Sat

5 min brisk walk, two build-ups at 60 percent, then:

5-6
100 m sprints at 85-90 percent
Walk back fully between reps. Breathe: hard exhales while sprinting, slow nose breathing in recovery. Wrong if hamstrings feel tight before a rep; skip that session. Never sprint cold.
Sprints keep the heart strong and spike growth hormone without burning the surplus that jogging would. Sunday: flow plus an easy 15 minute walk.

The Week at a Glance

Push-Pull-Legs twice, one athlete day. Beat your logbook in round two. Tap a day to open it.

Every 4th week, cut all sets by one third. Deload weeks are when joints recover and growth catches up. Mark it in your calendar now.

The Rules That Matter

01
Protein at every meal
1.6 to 2 g per kg bodyweight daily. Vegetarian arsenal: paneer, dal, soy chunks, milk, Greek yogurt, peanuts, tofu. The surplus only builds muscle if protein shows up.
02
Sleep 7.5 to 9 hours
Muscle is built in bed. Treat bedtime like a training session.
03
Never miss twice
One missed session is life. Two in a row is a broken habit. Protect the streak.
04
Log every set
Hit the top of a rep range on all sets and the next session gets harder: slower tempo, pause reps, or a harder variation. The logbook is the whole game.
05
Desk rule
Stand every 45 to 60 min: 10 chin tucks and one doorway stretch. Does more for your neck than any evening routine.
Eight weeks from now you will wish you had started today. You did. Keep showing up.
